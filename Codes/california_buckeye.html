Overview: California Buckeye (Aesculus californica)

- Below 4000 feet altitude

- Sierra Nevada, Coastal areas

- Up to 40 feet high and wide

Spring

- Candle shaped flowers - inflorescences
- Attract hummingbirds, moths, insects
- Nectar for butterflies

Summer

- Sheds leaves

- Becomes dormant

- Adapts to dry CA climate

Fall Season

- Pear-shape leathery fruit
- Brown nuts inside the fruit
- Toxic fruit and nut

Physiological Characteristics

- Compound Leaves
- Monoecious Flowers
- Capsular Fruit

Growing California Buckeye at Home

- Easy to grow
- Excellent fragrance (smell)
- Keep out of pet, children
